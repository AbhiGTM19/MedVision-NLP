# Cardiology Guidelines

## Common Conditions
- **Hypertension (HTN)**: High blood pressure, typically >130/80 mmHg. First-line treatments include ACE inhibitors, ARBs, Calcium Channel Blockers, or Thiazide diuretics.
- **Coronary Artery Disease (CAD)**: Narrowing of coronary arteries. Treatment includes statins, antiplatelets (e.g., Aspirin, Clopidogrel), beta-blockers.
- **Heart Failure (CHF)**: Inability of the heart to pump effectively. Management involves diuretics, beta-blockers, ACE/ARBs, and aldosterone antagonists.
- **Atrial Fibrillation (AFib)**: Irregular and often rapid heart rate. Anticoagulation (e.g., Apixaban, Rivaroxaban, Warfarin) is critical based on CHA2DS2-VASc score. Rate control via beta-blockers or calcium channel blockers.

## Critical Values & Interventions
- Troponin elevation indicates myocardial injury (e.g., MI).
- EKG showing ST elevation requires immediate reperfusion therapy (PCI or thrombolytics).
- BNP > 100 pg/mL suggests heart failure.
