# Neurology Guidelines

## Common Conditions
- **Cerebrovascular Accident (CVA) / Stroke**: Ischemic or hemorrhagic. Ischemic stroke management may include tPA if within the 4.5-hour window. Secondary prevention includes antiplatelets (Aspirin, Clopidogrel) and statins.
- **Epilepsy & Seizures**: Management includes antiepileptic drugs (AEDs) such as Levetiracetam, Valproate, Phenytoin, or Lamotrigine.
- **Migraines**: Acute treatment with Triptans or NSAIDs. Prophylaxis with beta-blockers, Topiramate, or CGRP antagonists.
- **Parkinson's Disease**: Degeneration of dopaminergic neurons. Mainstay of treatment is Levodopa/Carbidopa. Dopamine agonists and MAO-B inhibitors are alternatives.
- **Multiple Sclerosis (MS)**: Autoimmune demyelination. Acute flares treated with high-dose corticosteroids. Disease-modifying therapies (DMTs) like Ocrelizumab or Interferons for long-term management.

## Diagnostic Procedures
- MRI Brain is the gold standard for soft tissue evaluation (e.g., tumor, MS, stroke evolution).
- Non-contrast CT Head is the initial test of choice to rule out acute hemorrhage in stroke protocols.
- EEG for evaluating seizure activity.
- Lumbar Puncture (LP) for meningitis or MS (oligoclonal bands).
